# Israeli Tech Companies - Interview Guide

Detailed interview processes for top Israeli tech companies. Includes typical rounds, question types, tech stacks, and cultural expectations.

**Last updated**: Q2 2026

---

## Tier 1: Israeli Public Companies / Unicorns

### Wix

- **HQ**: Tel Aviv
- **Focus**: Website building platform, e-commerce, SaaS
- **Tech stack**: React, Node.js, Scala, Java, MongoDB, Kubernetes, custom rendering engines
- **Team size**: 5,000+ employees
- **Interview process** (4-5 stages, 3-4 weeks):
  1. HR phone screen (20 min): Motivation, team fit, salary expectations
  2. Take-home assignment (4-6 hours): Build a small app or component. Frontend: React app with specific UX requirements. Backend: API design + implementation.
  3. Technical interview #1 (60 min): Review take-home, deep dive into decisions, live coding extension
  4. Technical interview #2 (60 min): System design (Senior+) or algorithm problem (Junior/Mid)
  5. Team lead / hiring manager (45 min): Behavioral, culture fit, career goals
- **What they look for**: Product thinking, clean code, performance awareness, collaborative mindset
- **Tips**: Wix values "ownership" heavily. Prepare stories about owning features end-to-end. Their codebase is massive, so show comfort with large codebases.

### Monday.com

- **HQ**: Tel Aviv
- **Focus**: Work OS / project management platform
- **Tech stack**: React, Ruby on Rails, Node.js, PostgreSQL, Redis, GraphQL, Kubernetes
- **Team size**: 1,800+ employees
- **Interview process** (4 stages, 2-3 weeks):
  1. HR screen (20 min)
  2. Technical phone screen (45 min): Live coding on CoderPad, medium difficulty
  3. On-site day (3-4 hours): 2 technical rounds (coding + system design) + 1 behavioral
  4. Final round with hiring manager (30 min)
- **What they look for**: Speed of execution, ability to handle real-time systems, collaborative culture
- **Tips**: Monday.com interviews are fast-paced. Practice solving problems quickly. They care a lot about real-time collaboration, so prepare for WebSocket/event-driven architecture questions.

### Check Point

- **HQ**: Tel Aviv
- **Focus**: Cybersecurity (network, cloud, mobile, endpoint)
- **Tech stack**: C, C++, Python, Java, Go, Linux kernel, networking protocols
- **Team size**: 7,000+ employees
- **Interview process** (4-5 stages, 3-4 weeks):
  1. HR screen (20 min)
  2. Technical phone screen (60 min): Coding + networking/OS fundamentals
  3. On-site: 2-3 technical rounds (algorithm, system design, domain knowledge)
  4. Team lead interview (45 min)
  5. VP/Director interview (Senior+ only)
- **What they look for**: Deep systems knowledge, security mindset, protocol understanding
- **Tips**: Brush up on networking (TCP/IP, HTTP, TLS), Linux internals, and common attack vectors. Check Point interviews often test low-level knowledge that other companies skip.

### CyberArk

### Mobileye (NASDAQ: MBLY, majority-owned by Intel)

- **HQ**: Jerusalem
- **Focus**: Autonomous driving, ADAS, computer vision
- **Tech stack**: C++, Python, CUDA, deep learning frameworks (PyTorch/TensorFlow), real-time systems
- **Team size**: 3,000+ employees
- **Interview process** (5 stages, 3-5 weeks):
  1. HR screen (20 min)
  2. Technical phone screen (60 min): Coding + math/algorithm focus
  3. On-site day: 3-4 rounds (coding, system design, domain-specific)
  4. Presentation round (Senior+): Present a past project or solve a take-home problem
  5. Final interview with director
- **What they look for**: Strong algorithms, mathematical thinking, real-time systems knowledge, C++ proficiency
- **Tips**: Mobileye interviews are among the most technically demanding in Israel. Prepare for optimization problems, embedded systems constraints, and computer vision fundamentals.

## Tier 3: Multinational R&D Centers

### Google Israel

- **HQ**: Tel Aviv, Haifa
- **Focus**: Various (Cloud, Waze, Search, Ads, YouTube)
- **Interview process**: Standard Google process (5-7 stages over 4-8 weeks)
  - 1 phone screen + 4-5 on-site rounds
  - Coding (2-3 rounds), system design (1-2 rounds), behavioral (Googleyness)
- **Tips**: Prepare with LeetCode medium-hard problems. System design at Google scale (billions of users). The bar is very high, but the process is well-documented online. Waze team interviews may focus more on real-time data and geo problems.

### Meta (Facebook) Israel

- **HQ**: Tel Aviv
- **Focus**: Various (security, infrastructure, AI)
- **Interview process**: Standard Meta process (4-5 stages)
  - 2 coding rounds, 1 system design, 1 behavioral
- **Tips**: Meta coding interviews are fast-paced (2 medium problems in 45 min). Practice speed. System design focuses on social graph and distributed systems.

### Amazon Israel

- **HQ**: Tel Aviv, Haifa
- **Focus**: AWS, Alexa, retail
- **Interview process**: Standard Amazon loop (5-6 stages)
  - Coding, system design, behavioral (Leadership Principles heavy)
- **Tips**: Amazon interviews revolve around Leadership Principles. Prepare STAR-format stories for each principle. Technical coding is LeetCode medium. System design focuses on distributed systems.

### Microsoft Israel

- **HQ**: Herzliya, Tel Aviv
- **Focus**: Azure, Office, security, R&D
- **Interview process** (4-5 stages):
  - Phone screen + 3-4 on-site rounds (coding, design, behavioral)
- **Tips**: Microsoft interviews are generally more relaxed than other FAANG. Focus on practical coding and system design. .NET knowledge is a plus for some teams but not required.

### Apple Israel

- **HQ**: Herzliya
- **Focus**: Hardware-software integration, Siri, health, chip design
- **Interview process** (5+ stages, can be very long):
  - Multiple phone screens + on-site day
  - Very team-specific
- **Tips**: Apple interviews vary greatly by team. C/C++ and systems programming are common requirements. Apple values secrecy, so do not ask about specific projects during the interview.

## General Interview Tips for Israeli Tech

### Culture and Communication
- **Language**: Most interviews are in a mix of Hebrew and English. Technical terms are usually in English, but casual discussion is in Hebrew. Ask at the start which language the interviewer prefers.
- **Informality**: Israeli interviews are less formal than US ones. First names are used universally. Interviewers may be late. This does not reflect on you.
- **Argumentation**: It is normal and expected to discuss, debate, and even push back on the interviewer's suggestions. This shows critical thinking.
- **Honesty**: If you do not know something, say so directly. Trying to bluff is seen very negatively.

### Compensation Discussion
- **Timing**: Salary is often discussed in the first HR call. Be prepared with a range.
- **Do not undersell**: Israeli tech pays well, and companies expect negotiation.
- **Ask about benefits**: Keren Hishtalmut, pension rates, and car allowance are standard negotiation points.

### Post-Interview
- **Timeline**: Israeli companies often move fast. Expect feedback within 1-2 weeks.
- **Counter-offers**: Common in the market. Companies are used to competing for candidates.
- **Multiple processes**: Running 2-3 interview processes simultaneously is standard and expected.
