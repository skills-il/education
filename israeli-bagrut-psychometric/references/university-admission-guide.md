# Israeli University Admission Guide

## Overview

Israeli university admissions are primarily based on a combined score called the **Sekhem** (ציון סכם), which merges the Bagrut matriculation average with the Psychometric Entrance Test (PET) score. Each university and program has its own weighting formula and minimum thresholds.

## Admission Tracks

### Standard Track (Bagrut + Psychometric)
- Most common path
- Sekhem = weighted combination of Bagrut average and PET score
- Each university calculates the "optimal" combination that maximizes applicant's score

### Psychometric-Only Track
- For students with exceptional PET scores (typically 700+)
- Available at some universities for some programs
- Does not require a full Bagrut certificate
- Usually requires higher PET score than the standard track cutoff

### Mechina (Preparatory Program)
- For students without a full Bagrut or with low scores
- Typically 1-year program
- Successful completion may qualify for university admission
- Available at most universities

### Foreign Credentials Track
- For students with non-Israeli high school diplomas
- Accepts IB, AP, A-Levels, SAT, and other international credentials
- Each university has specific conversion tables

## Tips for Maximizing Admission Chances

1. **Take the PET multiple times** - universities use your highest score
2. **Universities calculate "optimal" scores** - they try different weightings and use the one that benefits you most
3. **5-unit Bagrut subjects matter** - bonus points significantly boost the Bagrut average
4. **Apply to multiple programs** - admission cutoffs vary between universities
5. **Consider the Mechina route** - if scores are borderline, a preparatory year can help
6. **Check specific requirements** - some programs require specific Bagrut subjects (e.g., Chemistry for Medicine)
