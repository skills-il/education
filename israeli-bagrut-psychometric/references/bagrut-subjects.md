# Complete Guide to Bagrut Subjects

This reference provides a comprehensive list of Bagrut (בגרות) examination subjects, study unit levels, mandatory versus elective classification, and minimum requirements for obtaining a full Bagrut certificate (te'udat bagrut, תעודת בגרות).

## Minimum Requirements for Full Certificate

To receive a te'udat bagrut, a student must:

1. Accumulate at least **21 study units** (yechidot limud, יחידות לימוד) across all subjects
2. Pass all mandatory subjects at the required minimum unit levels
3. Complete at least **one subject at 5 study units** (chamesh yechidot, חמש יחידות)
4. Achieve a **passing grade of 56%** (ציון עובר) or higher in every exam
5. Complete a community service requirement (ma'aseh chessed, מעשה חסד) of at least 40 hours

## Mandatory Subjects (Miktzo'ot Chova, מקצועות חובה)

The following subjects are required for every student pursuing a Bagrut certificate.

| Subject | Hebrew Name | Minimum Units | Notes |
|---------|-------------|---------------|-------|
| Mathematics (Matematika) | מתמטיקה | 3 | Can be taken at 3, 4, or 5 units |
| English (Anglit) | אנגלית | 3 | Can be taken at 3, 4, or 5 units |
| Hebrew Language and Literature (Lashon v'Sifrut) | לשון וספרות | 2 | Internal + external exams |
| Bible Studies (Tanakh) | תנ"ך | 2 | Content varies by school sector |
| History (Historia) | היסטוריה | 2 | OR Civics at 2 units (see below) |
| Civics (Ezrachut) | אזרחות | 2 | All students take Civics; History may satisfy the 2-unit social studies requirement depending on school track |
| Hebrew Composition (Habaat Ktiva) | הבעה בכתב | 2 | Writing proficiency exam |
| Physical Education (Chinuch Gufani) | חינוך גופני | Internal assessment | No external Bagrut exam |

### Notes on Mandatory Subjects

- **Mathematics**: The 3-unit level is the minimum. Students aiming for competitive university programs should consider 4 or 5 units. The 5-unit math exam provides the highest bonus points (up to 35) for university admission.
- **English**: Similarly, 3 units is the minimum. The 5-unit English exam provides up to 25 bonus points.
- **Civics**: All students study Civics. In some tracks, it counts toward the social studies requirement alongside History.
- **Bible Studies**: In secular (mamlachti) schools, this covers selected Tanakh texts. In religious (mamlachti-dati) schools, the curriculum is expanded with additional commentary and Talmud components.

## Study Unit Levels Explained

| Level | Hebrew | Description | Bonus Points for University |
|-------|--------|-------------|----------------------------|
| 1 Unit | יחידה אחת | Introductory, rarely used for Bagrut | No bonus |
| 2 Units | שתי יחידות | Basic level for some mandatory subjects | No bonus |
| 3 Units | שלוש יחידות | Standard level, minimum for Math and English | No bonus |
| 4 Units | ארבע יחידות | Enhanced level | Up to 12.5 bonus points per subject |
| 5 Units | חמש יחידות | Advanced level, highest difficulty | Up to 20-35 bonus points per subject |

### Bonus Points by Subject (5-Unit Level)

| Subject | Maximum Bonus Points | Notes |
|---------|---------------------|-------|
| Mathematics | 35 | Highest bonus of any subject |
| English | 25 | Second highest bonus |
| Physics | 25 | High-value science |
| Chemistry | 25 | High-value science |
| Biology | 20 | Standard science bonus |
| Computer Science | 20 | Growing recognition |
| Other 5-unit subjects | 20 | Standard bonus |

**Bonus points for 4-unit subjects**: Up to 12.5 points per subject, applied by universities.

## Exam Structure

Each Bagrut exam has two components:

| Component | Hebrew | Weight | Description |
|-----------|--------|--------|-------------|
| External Exam (Bchina Chitzonit) | בחינה חיצונית | 70% | Nationally standardized, graded externally |
| Internal Assessment (Magen) | מגן | 30% | School-based assessment, teacher-graded |

**Final grade formula**: (External Exam x 0.70) + (Internal Assessment x 0.30)

## Retaking Exams

- Students who fail (below 56%) may retake in the next moed (testing period)
- Retake exams (moed bet, מועד ב') follow the same format
- The higher score replaces the lower one
- No limit on retake attempts
- Past exams (bchinat avar) are published by the Ministry of Education for practice
