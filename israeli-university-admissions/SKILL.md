# Israeli University Admissions

## Instructions

### Step 1: Understand the Israeli Higher Education System

**Institution Types:**

| Type | Hebrew Name | Count | Accreditation |
|------|-----------|-------|--------------|
| Research Universities | אוניברסיטאות מחקריות | 9 | Malag (Council for Higher Education) |
| Academic Colleges | מכללות אקדמיות | ~30 | Malag |
| Teacher Training Colleges | מכללות לחינוך | ~20 | Malag |
| Open University | האוניברסיטה הפתוחה | 1 | Malag, open admissions |

**Research Universities (sorted by founding year):**
1. Technion (Technion - Israel Institute of Technology), Haifa, 1912
2. Hebrew University of Jerusalem (Ha'Universita Ha'Ivrit), 1918
3. Weizmann Institute of Science, Rehovot, 1934 (graduate only)
4. Bar-Ilan University, Ramat Gan, 1955
5. Tel Aviv University, Tel Aviv, 1956
6. University of Haifa, Haifa, 1963
7. Ben-Gurion University of the Negev, Be'er Sheva, 1969
8. Ariel University, Ariel, 2012 (university status)
9. Reichman University (IDC Herzliya), Herzliya, 1994 (university status 2021)

**Malag (HaMoatza Le'Haskalah Gvoha):**
The Council for Higher Education is the sole accrediting body for Israeli higher education institutions. Only Malag-accredited degrees are recognized by the State. Programs must be approved before they can accept students.

### Step 2: Explain the Psychometric Entrance Test

**PET (Psychometric Entrance Test) / Mirkam (computerized version):**

The Psychometric exam is administered by NITE (National Institute for Testing and Evaluation, HaMerkaz Ha'Artzi Li'Vchinat v'Ha'arachot). It is the primary standardized test for university admissions in Israel.

**Test Structure:**

| Section | Hebrew Name | Duration | Content |
|---------|-----------|----------|---------|
| Verbal Reasoning | Milulim/Chashiva Milutit | ~20 min x 2 | Sentence completion, analogies, reading comprehension, logic |
| Quantitative Reasoning | Kamutim/Chashiva Kamutit | ~20 min x 2 | Algebra, geometry, graphs, word problems |
| English | Anglit | ~20 min x 2 | Vocabulary, sentence completion, reading comprehension |

**Scoring:**
- Each section: 50-150 scale
- Total composite: 200-800 scale
- Mean score: approximately 540
- Score is standardized (percentile-based)
- Scores valid for 7 years (as of current policy)

**MIRKAM (computerized PET):**
- Same content areas as paper PET
- Administered on computers at testing centers
- Adaptive elements in some sections
- Results available faster (approximately 35 days)
- Offered multiple times per year (typically 5-6 dates)

**Key preparation strategies:**
1. Practice with official NITE sample tests (available on their website)
2. Focus on speed and accuracy, especially in quantitative section
3. For verbal: build Hebrew vocabulary systematically (milim nirdafot, milim hofchiyot)
4. For English: read academic-level English texts daily
5. For quantitative: review basic math concepts (no calculator allowed)
6. Time management is critical; learn when to skip a question

### Step 3: Calculate the Sekher Kabala (Admission Score)

The Sekher Kabala is a weighted composite score used by universities for admissions decisions. Each university calculates it differently, but the general formula:

**Standard Calculation:**
```
Sekher Kabala = (Bagrut Average x Weight_B) + (Psychometric Score x Weight_P)
```

**Typical Weightings by Institution:**

| University | Bagrut Weight | Psychometric Weight | Notes |
|-----------|--------------|-------------------|-------|
| Hebrew University | ~50% | ~50% | Varies by faculty |
| Tel Aviv University | ~50% | ~50% | Some faculties use multi-factor |
| Technion | ~40% | ~60% | Engineering: strong math emphasis |
| Ben-Gurion University | ~50% | ~50% | Flexible for periphery students |
| Bar-Ilan University | ~50% | ~50% | Standard weighting |
| University of Haifa | ~50% | ~50% | Varies by department |

**Bagrut Average for Sekher Kabala:**
- Raw grades are weighted by yechidot (study units)
- Bonus points: +25 for 5-yechidot subjects, ~+12.5 for 4-yechidot
- The weighted average can exceed 100 due to bonus points
- Formula: Sum(grade_i x units_i + bonus_i) / Sum(units_i)

**Example Calculation:**
```
Student has:
- Math 5 units: grade 85 + 25 bonus = 110 (weighted: 110 x 5 = 550)
- English 5 units: grade 90 + 25 bonus = 115 (weighted: 115 x 5 = 575)
- History 2 units: grade 78 (weighted: 78 x 2 = 156)
- Civics 2 units: grade 82 (weighted: 82 x 2 = 164)
- Bible 2 units: grade 75 (weighted: 75 x 2 = 150)
- Hebrew 2 units: grade 80 (weighted: 80 x 2 = 160)

Total weighted: 550+575+156+164+150+160 = 1,755
Total units: 5+5+2+2+2+2 = 18
Bagrut Average = 1,755 / 18 = 97.5

If Psychometric = 680:
Sekher = (97.5 x 0.5) + (680 x 0.5) = 48.75 + 340 = 388.75
(Actual formulas vary; some normalize to different scales)
```

### Step 4: Guide Through Application Timeline

**Typical Academic Year Admissions Timeline:**

| Month | Activity | Details |
|-------|----------|---------|
| October-November | Applications open | Minhal HaStudentim (Student Administration) begins accepting |
| December-January | Early decision | Some programs offer early acceptance |
| January-March | Psychometric tests | Winter testing dates |
| March-April | Application deadline | Most universities close applications |
| April-June | Psychometric tests | Spring/summer testing dates |
| May-July | Acceptance notices | Rolling admissions decisions |
| July-August | Moed Bet Psychometric | Last chance to improve scores |
| September-October | Orientation (Shavua Klitah) | New student week |
| October | Academic year begins | Semester Alef starts |

**Application Requirements (Typical):**
- Completed application form (online via university portal)
- Te'udat Bagrut or equivalent (certified copy)
- Psychometric score (sent directly by NITE)
- Teudat Zehut copy
- Application fee (approximately 300-400 NIS per institution)
- For specific programs: portfolio (art/architecture), interview (medicine, law), additional exam (music)

### Step 5: Alternative Pathways

**Mechina Kdam-Akademit (Pre-Academic Preparatory Program):**
- For students without a full Te'udat Bagrut or below minimum admission scores
- Duration: 1 academic year (October-June)
- Offered by most universities and some colleges
- Successful completion is equivalent to Te'udat Bagrut for admission purposes
- Some Mechina programs specialize: science track, humanities track, or combined

**Atuda (Military Academic Reserve Program):**
- Study before military service (deferred service)
- Available for in-demand fields: engineering, medicine, sciences, computer science
- Student studies 3-4 years, then serves as officer in relevant field for 4-6 years
- Highly competitive; strong academic record required
- Covers tuition and provides a stipend

**Shnot Sherut (Service Year):**
- National service or military service before university
- Can improve admission chances through supplemental scores or affirmative action
- Soldiers (chayyalim meshuchrarim) often receive tuition discounts and extra support

**Open University (Ha'Universita Ha'Petuchah):**
- No entrance requirements; open admissions policy
- Can transfer credits to other universities after proving academic ability
- Especially suitable for working students or those wanting to demonstrate academic capability

**External Exams (Bchinat Masav):**
- Administered by some universities as alternatives to standard admissions
- Subject-specific assessments, particularly for graduate programs

### Step 6: Program-Specific Requirements

**Medicine (Limmudei Refuah):**
- Highest admission requirements in Israel
- Typical minimum: Sekher Kabala ~730-750+ (varies by year and institution)
- Additional: multi-mini interview (MMI) or personal interview
- Biology and Chemistry at 5-yechidot Bagrut strongly recommended
- Programs: Hebrew University-Hadassah, Tel Aviv University, Technion, Ben-Gurion, Bar-Ilan

**Law (Mishpatim):**
- High Sekher Kabala required (typically 680-720+)
- No specific Bagrut subject prerequisites
- Hebrew University, Tel Aviv University, Bar-Ilan, University of Haifa, Reichman

**Computer Science (Mada'ei HaMachshev):**
- Math at 5-yechidot Bagrut typically required
- Strong Psychometric quantitative score important
- Available at all research universities and many colleges

**Engineering (Handasa):**
- Technion is the flagship; also at Tel Aviv, Ben-Gurion, Ariel, and academic colleges
- Math and Physics at 5-yechidot Bagrut required
- Quantitative Psychometric section heavily weighted

## Examples

### Example 1: Sekher Kabala Calculation
**Input**: "My Bagrut average is 95, and I got 650 on the Psychometric. Can I get into Tel Aviv University computer science?"
**Output**: Based on typical Tel Aviv University CS admission data: with a 50/50 weighting, your estimated Sekher Kabala is approximately (95 x 0.5) + (650 x normalized) which is competitive for CS. The typical cutoff for TAU CS has been around Sekher Kabala 660-680 in recent years. Your Bagrut is excellent; the Psychometric is solid. You are likely competitive, but exact cutoffs change each year based on the applicant pool. Consider applying early and including a second-choice department as backup.

### Example 2: Alternative Pathway
**Input**: "I don't have a Te'udat Bagrut. Can I still go to university?"
**Output**: Yes, several pathways exist: (1) Mechina Kdam-Akademit: a one-year preparatory program offered at most universities that substitutes for Bagrut upon completion. (2) Open University: enroll in courses with no prerequisites, accumulate credits, then transfer to a regular university. (3) Complete Bagrut as an external examinee (nivchan chitzoni) through Misrad HaChinuch. (4) Some academic colleges have more flexible admission criteria. Recommended: contact the Minhal HaStudentim at your target university for personalized guidance.

### Example 3: Psychometric Preparation
**Input**: "I'm taking the Psychometric in 2 months. How should I prepare?"
**Output**: 8-week preparation plan: Weeks 1-2: diagnostic practice test + review fundamentals in all three sections (verbal, quantitative, English). Weeks 3-4: intensive quantitative review (algebra, geometry, data interpretation) as this section has the clearest improvement potential. Weeks 5-6: verbal reasoning strategies (elimination technique, analogy patterns) + English vocabulary building. Weeks 7-8: full-length timed practice tests (at least 3), review mistakes, focus on time management. Key tip: the NITE website offers free sample tests (dugma'ot) that mirror the real exam format exactly.

## Troubleshooting

- **Issue**: Student has a low Psychometric score but high Bagrut
  **Solution**: Some universities allow Bagrut-only (mishkalot) admission for students with very strong Bagrut averages (typically 100+ with bonuses). Also, retaking the Psychometric is allowed (no limit on attempts; best score counts). Consider MIRKAM (computerized version) for faster results.

- **Issue**: Student unsure which university to apply to
  **Solution**: Consider: (1) program ranking and reputation in the specific field, (2) geographic preference and commute, (3) tuition and scholarship availability (milgot), (4) campus culture and size, (5) job placement rates for the program. Note that Israel has no official university ranking system; reputation varies by field.

- **Issue**: Student is an oleh (immigrant) or returning resident
  **Solution**: Special accommodations exist: extended time on Psychometric (25% extra), Hebrew ulpan credits, dedicated Mechina programs for olim, potential tuition discounts from the Ministry of Aliyah and Integration (Misrad Ha'Aliyah V'Ha'Klita). Contact the university's Olim coordinator.
