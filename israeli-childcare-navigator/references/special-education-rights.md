# Israeli Special Education: Rights and Procedures

## Legal Framework

| Law | Year | Key Provisions |
|-----|------|----------------|
| Compulsory Education Law | 1949 | Free compulsory education for all children |
| Special Education Law | 1988 | Establishes right to free special education ages 3-21 |
| Pupils' Rights Law | 2000 | General student protections |
| Amendment 11 to Special Education Law | 2018 | Major reform: renamed committee, gave parents framework choice, updated terminology |

## Va'adat Zakaut V'Iyun (Eligibility and Characterization Committee)

Amendment 11 (2018) renamed the old "Va'adat Hashama" (placement committee) to "Va'adat Zakaut V'Iyun". The colloquial name "va'adat hashama" is still widely used.

### Who's on the committee
- Ministry of Education official with special education expertise (chair)
- Psychologist
- Social worker
- Special education supervisor
- Sometimes: physicians, therapists

### What the committee decides
- Whether the child is eligible for special education services
- The service basket (sal sherutim) allocated
- How many support hours to allocate
- Which specific services the child receives

### What the committee does NOT decide (since Amendment 11)
- **Placement:** Parents now choose the educational framework (regular school, special ed class in regular school, or special school)
- Diagnosis (that comes from a separate evaluation)
- The decisions are not irreversible (can be appealed via "hashaga", and annual review is a right)

## Placement Continuum (Least to Most Restrictive)

| Level | Setting | Typical Support |
|-------|---------|-----------------|
| 1 | Regular class with support | 3-12 hours/week of specialist services |
| 2 | Integration class (kita meshulevet) | Mixed-ability, co-taught by regular and special ed teachers |
| 3 | Special education class in regular school | 6-12 students, specialized curriculum |
| 4 | Specialized school (beit sefer meyuchad) | Full-day with integrated therapies |
| 5 | Therapeutic framework | Highly specialized for emotional/behavioral needs |

## Support Hours by Severity

| Severity Level | Weekly Hours | Examples |
|---------------|--------------|---------|
| Light | 3-5 | Learning disabilities, mild speech delays |
| Moderate | 6-12 | Autism spectrum (level 1), ADHD with comorbidities |
| Significant | 13-20 | Autism spectrum (level 2), intellectual disabilities |
| Profound | 21+ | Multiple disabilities, severe developmental delays |

## Available Therapeutic Services

- Speech and language therapy (klinaot tikshoret)
- Occupational therapy (ripui be'isuk)
- Physical therapy (fizioterapia)
- Psychological services
- Art/music/movement therapy
- Assistive technology
- Shadow aide (saya/sayat) -- for significant needs only, not automatically provided

## Parental Rights (Strengthened by Amendment 11)

### Framework choice (NEW in Amendment 11)
- **Parents choose** the educational framework: regular school with inclusion, special ed class in regular school, or special education school
- The committee determines eligibility and service basket, but placement choice belongs to parents

### Before the committee meeting
- Right to receive written notice of the meeting date
- Right to see all documents that will be presented
- Right to submit your own evaluations and reports
- Right to request postponement (once) if not prepared

### During the committee meeting
- Right to attend and present your perspective
- Right to bring an advocate, lawyer, or professional
- Right to request that specific professionals attend

### After the committee meeting
- Right to receive the written decision within 2-4 weeks
- Right to file an objection (hashaga) to the regional committee
- Right to file a further objection to the national committee
- Right to request annual reevaluation
- Right to request change of framework at any time if circumstances change

## Objection Process (formerly "appeal")

Amendment 11 changed the terminology from "erer" (appeal) to "hashaga" (objection).

1. **First objection:** Regional committee (within 21 days of receiving decision)
2. **Second objection:** National committee (if regional objection denied)
3. **Legal action:** Administrative court (as last resort)

Tips for appeals:
- Document everything in writing
- Bring an education advocate (yoetz chinuchi) or special education attorney
- Present additional professional evaluations if available
- Focus on the child's best interest, not adversarial positioning

## Testing Accommodations

### Available for classroom tests
- Extended time: 25% or 50% additional time
- Reader assistance
- Scribe assistance
- Computer use
- Separate quiet testing room
- Breaks during exam
- Dictation of answers

### Available for Bagrut exams (with approval)
- Same accommodations as classroom, but requires formal approval
- Oral exams (rare, special cases only)
- Exam modifications (simplified language, reduced scope) -- requires separate approval process

## Evaluation Process

### School-based (free, slower)
1. Teacher identifies concerns
2. In-school intervention (3-6 months of documented support)
3. School psychologist initial assessment
4. Referral to Va'ada if intervention insufficient

### Private evaluation (2,000-5,000 NIS, faster)
1. Parent arranges private psychoeducational evaluation
2. Report submitted directly to Va'ada
3. Faster timeline but costs not covered by health funds for education purposes

### What a psychoeducational evaluation includes
- Cognitive assessment (IQ testing)
- Academic achievement testing
- Behavioral and emotional assessment
- Adaptive behavior evaluation
- Recommendations for support

## Geographic Variation

Service quality varies significantly:
- **Central Israel** (Tel Aviv, Ra'anana, Modi'in, Jerusalem): More comprehensive services, shorter waitlists, more private supplement options
- **Periphery** (Negev, Galilee, development towns): Longer waitlists, fewer specialists, less access to private alternatives
- **Arab sector**: Services available but often with longer wait times and fewer Arabic-speaking specialists

## Financial Support

Bituach Leumi provides disability allowance for children:
- Monthly stipend ranges from approximately 500-3,000+ NIS based on severity
- Separate application from the Va'ada process
- Based on medical diagnosis, not educational placement
