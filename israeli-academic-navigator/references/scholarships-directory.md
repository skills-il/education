# Israeli Scholarships Directory (Milgot)

## Overview

Israeli students have access to a wide range of scholarships (milgot, מלגות) from government, institutional, private, and nonprofit sources. This directory covers the major categories and specific programs available.

## Government and National Scholarships

### Perach (פר"ח) Tutoring Program

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Full Name | Project for Community Education (Peroject Chinuch Kehilati) |
| Award | Partial tuition waiver (approximately one-third of annual tuition) |
| Commitment | 4 hours per week of tutoring throughout the academic year |
| Who Qualifies | Most undergraduate students at accredited institutions |
| Registration | Through the Perach coordinator at your institution |
| Population Served | At-risk youth and children from disadvantaged backgrounds |
| Website | perach.org.il |
| Additional Benefits | Teaching experience, community service credit, professional development workshops |

### Atudot (עתודות) Pre-Army Academic Program

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Award | Full tuition, monthly stipend, living allowance |
| Commitment | Complete a degree before military service, then serve in the field of study (4-6 years) |
| Fields | Engineering, computer science, medicine, exact sciences, and more |
| Who Qualifies | Pre-IDF service high school graduates accepted to relevant programs |
| Application | Through IDF Human Resources (Mador Atudot) before enlistment |
| Notes | Highly competitive; requires strong Bagrut and interview |

### HESEG Foundation for Lone Soldiers

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Award | Full tuition, living expenses, laptop, mentoring |
| Who Qualifies | Lone soldiers (chayalim bodedim) who completed meaningful military service |
| Fields | All fields at accredited institutions |
| Application | During or immediately after military service |
| Website | heseg.org |

### Bituach Leumi (ביטוח לאומי) Student Benefits

| Benefit | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Reduced NI Payments | Full-time students pay reduced National Insurance rates |
| Student Exemptions | Students under age 21 may be fully exempt in certain cases |
| Documentation | Must submit proof of enrollment to Bituach Leumi annually |

## University-Specific Scholarships

### Merit-Based Scholarships (Milgot Mitztaynim, מלגות מצטיינים)

Every university offers merit-based awards for outstanding academic achievement.

| University | Scholarship Names | Award Range | Criteria |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------|----------|
| Hebrew University | Dean's Scholarship, Rector's Prize | 5,000-full tuition | GPA 90+, faculty recommendation |
| TAU | TAU Scholars, Faculty Awards | 5,000-full tuition | Top 5-10% of program |
| Technion | President's Scholarship, Faculty Awards | 5,000-full tuition | Academic excellence, research |
| BGU | Negev Scholarship, Dean's List Award | 3,000-full tuition | Top performers, periphery incentive |
| Bar-Ilan | BIU Scholars, Rector's Award | 5,000-full tuition | GPA-based, faculty specific |
| Haifa | Academic Excellence Award | 3,000-full tuition | Top 10% of each faculty |
| Ariel | President's Scholarship | 3,000-15,000 NIS | Academic performance |

### Need-Based Grants (Milgot Socio-Ekonomi'ot, מלגות סוציואקונומיות)

| University | Application Process | Criteria | Award Range |
|-----------|-------------------|----------|-------------|
| All universities | Through Dean of Students office (dekanat studentim) | Family income, number of dependents, distance from campus | Partial to full tuition |
| Application Period | Typically September-November for the academic year | Documentation required: tax returns, family status | Reviewed annually |

### Va'adot Siyua (ועדות סיוע) - Hardship Assistance Committees

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Purpose | Emergency financial assistance for students in acute hardship |
| How to Apply | Through the Dean of Students office (dekanat studentim) |
| Required Documents | Tax returns (tofes 106), bank statements, documentation of hardship |
| Award | Partial to full tuition reduction, sometimes additional living stipend |
| Timeline | Can be applied for at any point during the academic year |
| Renewal | Must reapply each year |

## How to Apply for Scholarships: Step-by-Step

1. **Start early**: Most university scholarship deadlines fall between September and November. Begin research in June-July.

2. **Visit the Dean of Students office (Dekanat Studentim, דקאנט סטודנטים)**: Every institution has a scholarships coordinator. Schedule a meeting to learn about all available options.

3. **Prepare documents**:
   - Tax returns (tofes 106, טופס 106) for yourself and parents if applicable
   - Bank statements (dapei cheshbon, דפי חשבון)
   - Military discharge certificate (teudat shichrur, תעודת שחרור)
   - Proof of oleh status if applicable (teudat oleh, תעודת עולה)
   - Academic transcripts
   - Letters of recommendation

4. **Apply to multiple scholarships**: There is no limit on the number of applications you can submit. Apply to every scholarship you may qualify for.

5. **Check external databases**:
   - Council for Higher Education (CHE/Malag) scholarship portal
   - Ministry of Education scholarship listings
   - University-specific scholarship pages
   - Non-profit organization websites

6. **Follow up**: After submitting applications, follow up with the scholarship office to confirm receipt and ask about timeline.
