# Israeli Universities and Colleges Comparison Guide

## Overview

Israel's higher education system includes 8 research universities, over 30 academic colleges (michlalot akademi'ot), and approximately 20 teacher training colleges (michlalot lechinuch). All degree-granting institutions are accredited by the Council for Higher Education (CHE, Malag, מל"ג).

This reference provides a side-by-side comparison of the major institutions to help students choose the right fit.

### Tuition Ranges (Annual, 2025-2026)

Tuition for BA programs at all universities is regulated by the CHE. The figures below are approximate and updated periodically.

| Degree Level | Regulated Annual Tuition | Notes |
|-------------|------------------------|-------|
| Bachelor's (BA/BSc) | 10,300-13,000 NIS | Regulated, mostly uniform across universities |
| Master's (MA/MSc, research track) | 12,000-16,000 NIS | Research-track MA is subsidized |
| Master's (professional/non-research) | 25,000-80,000 NIS | MBA, MPA, and other professional degrees vary widely |
| PhD | 0-5,000 NIS | Often waived entirely; many PhD students receive stipends |
| Medical degree (MD) | 10,300-13,000 NIS/year | Same regulated tuition, but 6-7 year program |

## Open University (Ha'Universita HaPtucha)

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Hebrew Name | האוניברסיטה הפתוחה |
| Location | Distance learning (main campus in Ra'anana) |
| Admission | Open enrollment, no entrance exams required |
| Tuition | Per course (approximately 2,500-3,500 NIS per course) |
| Degrees | BA and MA in multiple fields |
| Main Use Case | Working adults, career changers, students building credits to transfer to other universities |
| Transfer Path | Many students accumulate 30-60 credits then transfer to a research university |
| Study Format | Self-paced with periodic meetings, online resources |

## Choosing the Right Institution: Decision Framework

| If Your Priority Is... | Consider |
|------------------------|---------|
| Research and academia | Hebrew University, Technion, Weizmann (grad), TAU |
| Lowest cost of living | BGU (Be'er Sheva), Sapir (Sderot), Kinneret (Jordan Valley) |
| Largest social scene | TAU (30,000 students, Tel Aviv location) |
| Engineering excellence | Technion, Afeka, BGU |
| Art and design | Bezalel, Shenkar, HIT |
| Flexible admission | Open University (no requirements), colleges (lower thresholds) |
| English-language programs | Reichman (IDC), TAU International, Hebrew University Rothberg |
| Career in law | Hebrew University, TAU, Bar-Ilan, Haifa, Reichman |
| Medical studies | Hebrew University (Hadassah), TAU (Sackler), Technion (Rappaport), BGU (Goldman) |
| Periphery incentives | BGU, Sapir, Kinneret, Ariel (government grants and housing subsidies available) |
